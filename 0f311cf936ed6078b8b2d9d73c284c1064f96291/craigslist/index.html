Job Description

Craigslist is an American classified advertisements website that features a wide variety of categorized user generated posts and forums, founded by Craig Newmark in 1995. The company offers a “mostly free” service to publishers and target audience alike, wrapped in a simple yet crowded website.

Known worldwide and with categories for 70 countries, this website hasn’t seen drastic changes to its layout since the late 90’s. For this reason, I believe that it is important to modernize the plataform to cater to current users and bring along new ones.

Final Solution:

Phone App

Since Craigslist’s philosophy isn’t geared towards profit, their team’s focus is centered in developing a pragmatic tool. A very enginered endeavor that may have fallen short in considering the design as a mean to their end.

Despite having a mobile version of their site, the company does not have an official phone application, therefore many users are forced to access Craigslist’s information via 3rd party apps.

Among a selected group of users, one of the third party apps was presented for discussion. During the conversation, I asked the test subjects to point out the essential needs of the application and points that could be improved to maximize its usability. Summary

In summary, after following the design process, I’ve come to the conclusion that designing toward the simplification of the user’s interaction adds value to the product that Craigslist offers.

With that in mind, it was important to highlight the basic features of the site by keeping the main menu accessible at all times. This allows the user to search for items by keyword or category, keep their favorite items handy, manage their interactions and last but not least: submit their post. Additionally, touching the re-branded logo, allows the user to learn more about the company as well as manage their account and interactions.
