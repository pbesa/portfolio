Case Study

Craigslist App

Craigslist is an American classified advertisement website/app featuring an extensive variety of categorized user-generated posts and forums. The company offers a mostly free service to publishers and target audiences alike, wrapped in a simple yet crowded website.

Role

  • UX Designer
  • UI Designer
  • Interaction Designer

Problem

How might we make this app easier to use and engage users to keep using it?

The extensive categories and listings, make this application hard to understand and find what you're looking for, leading the user to confusion and frustration.

Solution

To make it as simple as possible, the decision of having the homepage with only two options (Search and Post), was based on the data collected from users, since these are the two main uses of the app. In addition, I decided to keep the navigation at the bottom of the app, to be accessible at all times.

Defining

Survey

I created a survey to obtain quantitative data that was completed by 20 people. Based on these findings, I took decisions about how to fix the main problems.

77.8%

Use Craigslist to buy or sell items.

40%

Reported to use Craigslist just a few times in the past or it's been a while since they used it.

45%

Were people between 30-39 years-old. And 40% were in between 18-29 years-old.

63.2%

Identified themselves as Caucasian.

Interview

I also asked the test subjects to point out what they like and dislike the most. To find the essential needs of the application and points that could be improved to maximize its usability. Here are some key insights:

"It's hard to trust listings as being real"

Most people said they feel there are a lot of scams and untrustworthy posts. Some are also concerned about their personal information to be exposed.

"No fee is taken from what is sold on Craigslist"

The majority of people like that it's a free app with local advertisements.

Personas

These personas are a representation of the typical Craigslist user. According to the survey above, almost all of the users are Millennials trying to either buy or sell items.

Branding

Logo

Since Craigslist’s main philosophy isn’t profit, its team is centered on developing a pragmatic tool. A very engineered endeavor that may have fallen short in considering the design as a means to their end.

This company hasn’t seen drastic changes to its layout since the late ’90s. For this reason, I believe that it is important to modernize the platform to cater to current users and bring along new ones, starting with its logo and overall feel.

Conclusion

Summary

In summary, after following the design process, I’ve concluded that designing toward the simplification of the user’s interaction adds value to the product that Craigslist offers.

With that in mind, it was important to highlight the basic features of the site by keeping the main menu accessible at all times. This allows the user to search for items by keyword or category, keep their favorite items handy, manage their interactions, and last but not least: submit their post. Additionally, touching the re-branded logo, allows the user to learn more about the company as well as manage their account and interactions.
